Admission

Elderly people want to live the rest of their lives and lives in the afternoons of life, in the afternoons of life, in the afterlife. Growing up children who have no desire to interfere with their children's progress and way of life have given up what they had in their hands when they were in distress. The situation of a lonely adult living with the loss of a spouse is still miserable in the day-to-day, when the elders in the newspapers and visual media are intimidated by violence, murder, intimidation, extortion, assault, etc. Sickness, forgetfulness, and recklessness can lead to anxiety in the home and a life of anxiety all the time. It is the duty of the society to overcome this situation and allow the elderly to live a comfortable life. This is necessary for the elderly, their associates, various organs of government, NGOs, and all of us. Against this backdrop, the International Panchayat Council has ratified the 1991 principles for the protection of senior citizens. Based on these principles, the Government of India has also enacted a number of schemes for the protection of senior citizens in Articles 41 and 46 of the Constitution of our country for the benefit of senior citizens.

Admission is open to any senior citizen of Indian origin who is neglected by their own children or free from family obligations and wants to serve the society for the rest of their lives due to loneliness or the absence of any family / person looking after him / her.
