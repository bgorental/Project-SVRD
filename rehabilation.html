SRI VAISHNAVI INTEGRATED REHABILITATION
CENTER FOR ADDICTS

Aims and Objectives

  • To create awareness and educate people about the ill-effects of alcoholism and substance abuse on the individual, the family, the workplace and society at large
  • To provide for the whole range of community based services for the identification, motivation, counseling, de addiction, after care and rehabilitation for whole person Recovery (WPR) of addicts to make a person drug free, crime free and gainfully employed
  • To alleviate the consequences of drug and alcohol dependence amongst the individual, the family and society at large
  • To facilitate research, training, documentation and collection of relevant information to strengthen the above mentioned objectives
  • To support other activities which are in consonance with the mandate of the Ministry of Social Justice & Empowerment in this field

    Target Groups

  • All victims of alcohol and substance (drugs) abuse
  • Children including street children, both in and out of school
  • Dependent women and young girls, affected substance abuse
  • High risk groups such as sex workers, Injecting Drug Users (IDUs), drivers etc.
  • Adolescents/youth

    • Individual Counselling

  • Counselling is a conscious, scientific process that is a dialogue between a person who is a problem consultant, who understands any problem of the individual and advises and guides them properly, or both. According to Petersen, "consultation is the process of helping the person to adapt to the environment and get out of the problem". Personal counselling is one of the most effective methods of addiction treatment. It is essential to first understand the causes of addiction, in order to make the addict free, the counsellor will know the reasons for the addiction by conducting a personal consultation and keeps these elements confidential. Only when a person understands the source of the problem will he be able to solve his problem. Personal counselling plays an important role in this regard.

    • Family counselling

  • Family counselling is a family or group consultation to solve a group or individual problem. A counsellor must first identify the source of the problem. To know the source of the problem, he or she must work with the individual and family. The whole family is in hardship due to one person's problems in the family and all members of the family are suffering. For example, if a person is addicted to drugs in a family, the family will suffer socially, psychologically, and financially. It is therefore advisable to do family counselling in addiction recovery centres for family members of the addict.

    • Group counselling

  • Group counselling is a type of consultation. "A consultation that brings together people who have the same problem and has the same mindset is called group counselling." This is how counselling is used in addiction care centres, where multiple people with the same problem are recorded to be addicted to addiction. Since all problems are the same, no consultation can be done. Many people's problems can be solved.

    • Behavioural counselling

  • Behavioural counselling is widely used in the treatment of debilitating mental illnesses, such as anxiety. Consultation for groups of the same behaviour or behaviour can be called behavioural counselling. It can be problematic and stressful. The consultant uses this consultation to change or modify behaviours in a step-by-step process using scientifically important techniques. It has been scientifically validated as a successful method of treating symptom.

Community services

Community services are provided at the community level to address the effects of drugs.

  • World Tobacco Day etc.
  • Awareness Programs (Distribution of Letters, Organizing Discussions in Communities, Awareness through Street Dramas).
  • Conduct seminars and seminars in school colleges.
  • Celebrate drug paraphernalia, (International anti-narcotics day).
  • Conducting surveys on drugs at the community level.
  • Identify areas of risk of drug addiction risk through surveys.
  • Promoting treatment for victims of addiction.
  • Eliminating superstitious beliefs in the community about addiction.
  • Health Checkups on Addiction.

    After care/follow-up

  • Post-treatment follow-up plays a very important role for drug addicts. This is because after being released from an addiction treatment centre, a person is more likely to get addicted to psychological, social, financial, family, and related problems. In such cases they will need counselling that will suggest self-reliance and appropriate compensation. They are told to take some medication for 2 years and then be taken to the addiction-free centre every month. If not found, contact with the home will be investigated for wellbeing. After treatment, addiction-free associations will be formed.

    • Recovery

  • "Recovery is the key to recovery for a person with a serious problem or disease and getting the best guidance and treatment for it," said Recovery. Because a person falls ill and loses his normal function and becomes abnormal. It's important to treat the person and make him come back to normal.
  • Addicted person is recovering from his addiction through personal therapy, family therapy, group therapy, behavioural therapy, and other activities.

    • Rehabilitation

  • Treating addicts after they are released is one of the most important tasks of addiction recovery centres, as the addict has suffered several losses during the addiction process. Such losses include family strife. Losing employment. Property damage, complaining from relationships and so on. In order to address these problems, addiction treatment centres will be provided with various facilities such as various associations, government departments, family counselling centres, vocational training centres, education centres, legal counselling centres and various governmental projects.
